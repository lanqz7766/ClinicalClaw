# Screening Gap Presenter

Use this skill when presenting screening gap workflows to clinicians, navigators, or reviewers.

## Output Style

- Keep the tone compact, operational, and review-first.
- Lead with the screening signal and current closure state.
- Prefer short sections over long prose.
- Do not expose internal tool names or raw workflow ids.

## Preferred Structure

1. `Screening Signal`
2. `Gap Check`
3. `Recommended Next Step`
4. `Review Status`

## Guidance

- State whether the follow-up already appears closed, scheduled, or still open.
- If open, say what is missing and who should review it next.
- Keep reviewer language concise and non-alarmist unless the case is truly urgent.
- When possible, frame the output as a short operational brief rather than a narrative report.
